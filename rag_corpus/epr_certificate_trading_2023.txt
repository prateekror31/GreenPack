TITLE: EPR Certificate Trading and Plastic Credit System — India 2023
SOURCE: CPCB Circular No. CPCB/EPR/2023/Plastic/001 (Mock Circular)

SECTION 1: Introduction to EPR Certificates
EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) certificates are tradeable instruments issued by the CPCB EPR portal to registered plastic waste processors (recyclers and co-processors) upon verification that a specified quantity of plastic waste has been collected and processed. One EPR certificate is equivalent to 1 metric tonne (1000 kg) of plastic waste processed. These certificates allow producers to demonstrate compliance with their EPR obligations without having to set up their own collection infrastructure.

SECTION 2: Who Can Generate EPR Certificates
EPR certificates can be generated by:
- Registered plastic waste recyclers (mechanical and chemical)
- Co-processors (cement kilns and other industrial facilities that use plastic waste as fuel)
- Waste-to-energy plants approved by CPCB
Recyclers must be registered on the CPCB EPR portal and must submit verifiable documentary evidence (weight slips, gate passes, consent orders) before certificates are issued.

SECTION 3: Certificate Validity and Expiry
EPR certificates issued in a given financial year (April 1 to March 31) are valid for that financial year plus the following two financial years. Certificates unused after 3 financial years expire and cannot be used to offset EPR obligations. Producers should maintain a certificate inventory and plan purchases to avoid certificate expiry.

SECTION 4: Price Discovery and Trading Mechanism
Certificate prices are determined by market forces on the CPCB portal. As of FY 2022-23, indicative prices ranged from INR 1,500 to INR 4,000 per metric tonne depending on plastic category and demand. Flexible plastic certificates typically trade at a premium because collection infrastructure for flexible plastic is less developed. Producers can buy certificates directly from recyclers through bilateral agreements or via the CPCB portal marketplace.

SECTION 5: How to Purchase EPR Certificates
Step 1: Log in to the CPCB EPR portal with your Producer ID.
Step 2: Navigate to the "EPR Credit Marketplace."
Step 3: Browse available certificates by plastic category.
Step 4: Select the quantity needed to cover your EPR shortfall.
Step 5: Complete payment through the portal's payment gateway.
Step 6: Certificates are credited to your EPR account within 2 working days of payment confirmation.

SECTION 6: Annual Reconciliation of EPR Obligations
At the end of each financial year, CPCB reconciles each producer's EPR obligation (based on total declared quantities for the year) against certificates purchased and recycling done. If a shortfall remains, CPCB issues an Environmental Compensation notice. The compensation amount is calculated as: Shortfall (tonnes) × Category-specific compensation rate (INR/tonne). Compensation rates are revised annually and published in the CPCB gazette notification.

SECTION 7: Interaction with State Pollution Control Boards
While CPCB oversees the national EPR portal, State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs) have authority to conduct physical audits of producers within their jurisdiction. SPCBs may request original ERP records, procurement invoices, and reconciliation worksheets during audits. Producers must cooperate with SPCB inspections and provide records within 7 working days of a formal request.
